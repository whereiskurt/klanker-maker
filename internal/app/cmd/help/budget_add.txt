Add budget (top-up) to a sandbox and auto-resume if suspended.

Increases the compute and/or AI budget limits for a sandbox by the
specified amounts. If the sandbox was suspended due to budget
exhaustion, it is automatically resumed after the top-up.

Usage:
  km budget add <sandbox-id> [flags]

Examples:
  # Add $10 to compute budget
  km budget add sb-a1b2c3d4 --compute 10.00

  # Add $5 to AI budget
  km budget add sb-a1b2c3d4 --ai 5.00

  # Top up both budgets at once
  km budget add sb-a1b2c3d4 --compute 25.00 --ai 10.00

Flags:
  --compute float   Amount in USD to add to the compute budget limit
  --ai float        Amount in USD to add to the AI budget limit

At least one of --compute or --ai must be specified.

What Happens:
  1. Current budget limits are read from DynamoDB
  2. New limits are calculated (additive — current + top-up)
  3. Updated limits are written to DynamoDB
  4. If sandbox was suspended (compute exhausted):
     - EC2 instances are started (or ECS tasks restarted)
  5. If AI access was revoked (AI budget exhausted):
     - Bedrock IAM policy is reattached to the sandbox role
  6. Summary is printed showing old and new limits

Auto-Resume:
  When a sandbox hits its budget limit, the budget-enforcer Lambda
  suspends it (stops EC2, revokes IAM). Adding budget via 'km budget add'
  detects this state and automatically resumes the sandbox — no manual
  intervention needed beyond the top-up.

Budget Tracking:
  Compute spend is tracked by the budget-enforcer Lambda (1-minute polls).
  AI spend is tracked by the http-proxy sidecar (real-time Bedrock
  request interception). Both are stored in the DynamoDB budget table.
